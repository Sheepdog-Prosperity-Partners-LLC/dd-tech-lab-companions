# Limitations

Prepared by Noah Green CPA CFE

This lab is a source-literacy tool. It does not produce legal conclusions, sanctions opinions, export classifications, anti-corruption findings, privacy opinions, financing recommendations, suitability recommendations, or jurisdiction-level scores.

## Global Limits

- Public records can be incomplete, stale, fragmented, jurisdiction-specific, or dependent on source-specific definitions.
- Machine translation can distort legal terms and entity forms.
- A no-hit result is usually inconclusive unless source coverage, query quality, identifier quality, and official-source scope support a narrower statement.
- Name matching is fragile across accents, abbreviations, entity suffixes, language variants, and transliteration.
- Identifier format validation proves only that the identifier shape passes a check, not that the entity is active, clean, or transaction-ready.
- Public procurement participation is not adverse by itself.
- Sanctions, debarment, and enforcement hits require source-specific reading and escalation.
- Securities filings are disclosures, not adverse events by themselves.
- Timeline outputs are source-routing aids. They do not interpret the legal effect of notices.
- The current repo uses synthetic data only and does not query live government systems.

## Brazil Limits

- A CNPJ format pass is not proof of Receita Federal registration status.
- A sanctions or debarment-style lead must be verified against the official record, including the sanctioning body, scope, date, and status.
- Procurement data can identify relationships to ask about, but it does not establish wrongdoing by itself.

## Argentina Limits

- CUIT/CDI format checks do not prove current registry status.
- Registro Nacional de Sociedades coverage and source-date notes must be read before relying on a no-hit or a stale-hit analysis.
- Boletin Oficial timeline outputs require notice-level review. Search results can miss notices or return context that requires local legal interpretation.
- BCRA public APIs can include sensitive financial-context surfaces. Do not publish personal or sensitive details.

## Escalation

Escalate to local counsel or specialist counsel when:

- a public record appears adverse
- a legal status must be interpreted
- a contract condition depends on the source
- sanctions, export, anti-money-laundering, anti-corruption, privacy, procurement, securities, or banking law may be implicated
- the result will affect closing, price, indemnity, escrow, termination rights, disclosure, or remediation
